February 2021

Figma Migration

We will be releasing a library for Figma on the week of Feb 15 - 19. All new and existing projects under our corporate account will have immediate access to them. The library requires no set-up. Things like fonts are also automatically taken care of through the platform. No need to be looking for the font files and color files anymore.

We will be running a workshop covering the basics: How to use Figma, how to structure projects moving forward. Sergej Spomer will be running these. Expect an event to pop in your calendars soon.

Design Tokens

We are changing how we are managing color. To allow a truly scalable theming architecture we are changing the way we name our variables and the color scale we have in the product. We are moving away from light-grey-3 in favor of a more descriptive definition based on use. Example: text-disabled.

You will see these changes implemented to our libraries from day one. These are also in alignment with our engineering teams and we are looking to centralize these even further to achieve 100% color accuracy in our implementation.

Feedback

One month after the migration completes. We will be running a satisfaction interview as we did a year ago. This is meant to understand any frustrations and keep improving our library. Make sure to spend some time giving feedback. Any questions regarding the migration can be addressed to #figma-in-transition https://nutanix.slack.com/archives/C01GKJEP9DF
